About the SIG

The Knowledge Banking Special Interest Group (KB-SIG), is a joint initiative of Internet Australia and Web Civics to define, refine and support means in which information management systems are defined as to support a knowledge economy built upon a foundation of primary beneficiaries and custodians of data relating to human activity being the human actor for whom the storage and use of data assets relate. These information management systems bring the human 'observer' as the custodian to active agents that are intended to work on their behalf. A Knowledge Banking Industry is envisaged to be the core-infrastructure to drive our knowledge economy. The creation of a Knowledge Banking Industry, requires new thinking, new 'ecosystems', defined inclusively to be 'fit for purpose' to support industry both here and abroad. The Knowledge Banking Special Interest Group (SIG) is being established to render meaningful support by way of community engagement and shared ownership, with a view to supporting the means in which Australians may be empowered to play a leadership role internationally, in the transition between an infosphere environment where data is universally held by incorporated entities to an environment where new alternatives are available, that are intended to improve support for personhood. The Wide-Spread comprehension that our outdated information management systems are causing significant problems in society, is disempowering unless we go about defining pragmatic solutions, that may in-turn result in the ubiquitous availability of new designs that solve otherwise impossible problems. The Knowledge Banking SIG is intended to render meaningful support to achieving this underlying goal, by way of a shared-values methodology that is centered upon the intersection between personhood, human rights and communications technology.

About Internet Australia

In preparing for the inaugural general meeting of the Internet Society of Australia ("Internet Australia") in 1996, Internet Luminary Geoff Huston said"This is the Society for everyone", "We want this body to be relevant to the very broad base of Internet users, publishers, developers and others who make the Internet what it is today. There are plenty of organisations for business; this one is for people. If you use the Internet and are interested in the things that affect its development in Australia and the world, the Internet Society can be your voice."

These visionary statements are of such greater importance today, than could ever have been reasonably considered when they were originally made. Membership to Internet Australia provides the means in which to participate in future making, vote on critical decisions and be made aware of 'what's going on' alongside other benefits. Membership is an important you can make your voice count, as we figure out how it is that we can make the internet work for you.

Our Values Based Approach

Our events program is designed to be inclusive as to support means in which all Australians are able to get involved in the design of our infosphere, as to undertake best efforts to define the world that will impact the future of our children. The Knowledge Banking SIG is being established upon a series of principles where the aim is to enshrine means in which social discussions are supported by Moral Grammer otherwise set-out in our system of democracy; which in-turn renders support for our shared commitments to international agreements, such as Universal Human Rights, which are often too hard for people to find meaningful utility when most in need. Knowledge Banking Industry systems are designed to support the means in which our system of government, is technically provided the means to use our communications systems to support us as they have been otherwise intended to do. The method of forming a Knowledge Banking Industry, is designed with the intent to support regulatory forms of government engagement by way of an emergent sector who are responsible to support principles such as 'rule of law', seperation of powers and the means to support individual personhood in our 'connected world'.

  • Meetings are open to adult members of society & we seek to ensure we build bridges using language that can be easily understood.
  • Every person has the right to be ruled by law. To realise this right the ability to have information available to us, that can be relied upon by others is imperative. Humans must be provided custodianship over the data generated as a result of their being.
  • Whilst the Primary Custodian for ones actions is ones self; The fact that there are pressures and costs does not absolve people of their moral responsibility
  • Artificial Intelligence (AI) designed to support humanity renders meaningful support as an extension of self. The Use of AI should not 'decieve, mislead' or distort communications relied upon, by others.
  • Information Technology is the primary means we observe the world and communicate with others. The tools defining humanity must be based on open-standards and should never be subject to express royality as a prerequisite to being entitled to human rights such as to include 'rule by law' as distinct to tyranny or man.
  • We are brothers and sisters of those who live abroad, our government has a rich heritage of contribution as having been part of the United Nations. Our efforts seek to inform our reprsentatives on matters that are relevent to us. To meaningfully support personhood of those overseas in addition to our own needs of personhood, as equipped through the use of technology.
  • We respect and tolerate differences of opinions and our shared common wealth of other cultures, and their communities, as we pay respect to one-another in good faith and fair dealings.
  • Our efforts are designed to uphold international human rights doctrine and to protect these important values by ensuring the design of information systems reflect the sentiments expressed in these internationally recgonised instruments. We, Humanity, are custodians for our natural world of which we have no alternative should we not value our world and those who depend upon it.

Background

In 2017, Australia Hosted the World Wide Web Conference, in Perth Australia. Web Civics presented "The Trust Factory 2017" where leading minds presented diverse views informing the foundations of what are our shared problems and opportunities, at the intersection between the infosphere, humanity and the natural world. The Knowledge Banking SIG is a follow-up program to make a different information management systems capability by way of defining a new industry of 'knowledge' or 'information fudicaries' that serve the people, much like a bank serves the people in the provision of bank-accounts as a financial fudicary. These events, are about ensure the manufacture of these systems, is undertaken in an inclusive manner.
